I'd like to dedicate the first thought on this site, to my best friend.

Varrow is the co-founder of the ELECTRIS Project as well as the person I trust the most.
What I'm about to tell you is about an idea he once had that unfortunately never came to be.

You see, Varrow wanted to create a streaming platform for indie content. A place fair to indie creators and film makers alike, dedicated to their work and compensating them for it far better than YouTube, with a user experience as great as on Netflix.

As if Netflix and YouTube had a baby, so to speak.
But it was far greater than just that.

He even wanted user-focused social features where people could have watch parties even with others without subscriptions.

He had this idea a while back, and presented it to fans of such content, creators and even potential investors.

He was shot down. Nobody liked the idea of his platform, well, nobody but me.
I loved his idea.

As I've recently starting making short films myself, I love the idea even more.
Unfortunately, he was in no shape to try to bring it to life after what he's been told.

I was certainly upset when he told me he was shutting down it's production, and maybe I didn't react in the best way at the time, but at heart, all I wanted was this project to exist. I'll say it again, I loved the idea. I love the idea. I want to see it one day. I don't know if anyone shares the same opinion as me, but I wanted to write this to show my buddy that at least someone believes in his idea, and that maybe he should give it another shot.

Thanks for reading.
